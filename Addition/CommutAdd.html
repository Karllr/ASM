The Commutative Property of Addition

What is 3 + 2?

It's 5, right? Well, you are right, and no one should tell you anything different. For example if you had 3 apples, and 2 oranges, you would have FIVE fruits.

So, 3 + 2 = 5
But what if we reverse the equation, like to the side?

2 + 3?

What is it?

2 + 3 = 5?

Really?

What is 2 + 3?

It's also 5? This can't be...
Go use a calculator and find out what 2 + 3 is
Oh. It is 5.

So, 2 + 3 is also 5
But there is something you should notice. Do you see it?

They're made of the same numbers!

Yes they are. Both equations use the numbers 2, 3, and 5!

So, 2 + 3, and 3 + 2, equal the same thing.

2 + 3 = 3 + 2

It's true!

5 + 6 = 6 + 5

7 + 9 = 9 + 7

Commutative Property

The Commutative Property

That was an example of the Commutative Property of Addition. This property states that if you switch the addends(numbers you are adding), in the equation, you will have the same answer. End of story, now learn your 6 addition tables!
